RAG Document Q&A

Ask questions about your own documents, get answers grounded in what they actually say. Chunking with overlap, sentence-transformer embeddings, FAISS similarity search, and a prompt that constrains the model to its retrieved context.

Top-k is the whole trade-off: more context usually means better answers, until the extra chunks are just noise the model has to ignore.

LLM Evaluation Harness

Scores answers on correctness, groundedness, and abstention — then fails CI when quality regresses. Heuristics run free on every commit; an LLM judge handles what they can't see.

Run it on a model that refuses everything and a model that hallucinates: the useless one scores higher. That's the case for never trusting a single metric.

MLOps Pipeline

Everything after model.fit() — MLflow-tracked training, a containerised serving API, and PSI drift monitoring on a schedule. Models don't fail loudly; accuracy just decays while nothing throws an exception.

Churn runs at 28%, so predicting "nobody leaves" scores 0.72 accuracy. The model's 0.75 is barely above a rock. Accuracy was the wrong thing to read.
